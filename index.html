My Journey

From my childhood I loved computers. I was always looking at my eldest brother's desktop computer. I learned many things only by observing him using his computer. The computer was like candy to a child to me.

When I was ten years old, my father taught me mathematics. Eventually, I loved mathematics and its problem-solving. When I finished high school, I started computer science and decided to learn everything with passion. My passion was the reason I had high scores in every subject. In the first semester, I learned about programming in the c++ language. Eventually, in the third semester, I started to learn about data structures and algorithms and implemented the following; Bubble sort, select sort, insert sort, stack, queue, singly linked list, and doubly linked. It was a great experience and was the beginning of my improvement in problem-solving. That is the reason I started to learn more about web development and software engineering.

After graduation, I could not find any job because of fewer opportunities in my country and nepotism. But, I did not give up and tried to implement my skills by practicing. For example, I used a website called CodeSignal to practice problem-solving skills. Additionally, I tried to build some web applications for people for free to keep myself busy with coding. And I did some freelancing projects in my local area. Nowadays, I am practicing front-end challenges on frontendmentor.io, which is a great website.

That was not the end. I met the Imagine Organization based in Germany. They are helping people to think better and helping them to get a job in European countries like Germany. I met my first volunteer coach, Annamaria Bero, at Imagine. She helped me to work on my CV and my LinkedIn profile. She is an outstanding coach and very friendly and inspiring. Her advice inspired me to become more active and work on my self-improvement.
